Introduction to Chords

Chords are any set of musical notes consisting of two or more musical notes played in harmony{together or at the ssme time}

Chords just as musical scale are essential in a guitar musical piece setting.Its harmony blends well into the background of a song and when set to play on it own,it usually steals the show

Chords in guitar are not to be ignored,as chord are used frequently in western music and other kinds of musical genre like west africa music,oceanic music,western classical music

Before a collection of note can be considered a chord,they have to relate,this relationship,can be based off the fact that the notes in the chord must be in the same scale

For example,A C major chord contain the notes,"C E G" in it.All these notes relate by their presence in the C major scale,"C D E F G A B"

CLASSIFICATION OF CHORDS

Chords can be classified based on the number of notes in them

1) Dyads - contains two notes,although its commonly called and classified as an interval by people who believe a chord should contain three or more notes. Examples include the power chord,tritone,ditone.

2) Traids - contains three notes,they are the most popular and most used chord class.Examples are these are the major chord,minor chord,augmented chord,diminished chord.

Tetrad/ Extended chord - usually contains four notes.Examples are the 6th,7th,9th,11th,13th chords.

Note : We could play chords with more than 4 notes in them but guitar chord at most can only have up to 4 notes due to the fact that we have just 4 fingers in the fretting hands,only in extreme cases can a guitarist play a 5 note chord by using the thumb of the fretting hand to hold down a note

Chord Formulae

Similar to the scale formula is the specific pattern of different chords.The formula is also as important as the scale formula as it serves the purpose of creating chords

Forming Chord With The Chord Formula

The rules for using the chord formula is the same as that used for the scale formula.Just little different

Rule 1

Chord are formed from a scale(usually the major scale),its the most commonly used and widely accepted scale for forming chord,the minor scale can also be used

Rule 2

A Chord formula usually have specified number of it notes / it follows the quantity of notes in the chord

Rule 3

A Chord formula usually have a fixed interval between its notes/ it follows the quality rule

Rule 4

It would finally follow the root note rule.The root note of a chord just like in the scale is the note upon which the other notes in the chords are built upon
