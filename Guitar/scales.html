What are Scales?

Scales are not only the building block of the sound,they are entities on which some or most of the other building block rely on,identify with and originate from partially,not enough for the scale to be the only building block of music but just enough for the other basic block to claim affliations with the scale so it be regarded as the first building block after the musical notes

Scales are series of musical notes {usually contains two or more notes} moving upward and downward with fixed intervals between each notes in such a way that when they are played together ,there is connection between those series of notes and they all fit together to be regarded as a body

Classification of Scales

Overwhelmed by the amount of scales we have??

Scales are majorly classified based on the numbers of their note

  1. chromatic scale- contains twelve musical notes in total . they includes the chromatic scale
  2. octatonic scale- contains eight musical notes . they include scales used in jazz and classical music
  3. heptatonic / diatonic scale - contains seven notes, they include major and minor scale
  4. hexatonic scale _ contains 6 notes,they include whole tones,augmented and blues scales
  5. pentatonic scale- they contain five scale, they include the major and minor pentatonic scale (used in rock music and some other musical genre)
  6. primitive music scale - these are groups of scale that are no longer in use and have becomed limited to primitve music and limited use in lithurgy
  7. tetratonic scale- contains 4 notes
  8. tritonic scale - contains 3 notes
  9. ditonic scale - contains 2 note
  10. monotonic scale - contains 1 note

Scale formula

The scale formula is the specific pattern and arrangement Of note in a given scale in which the scale can move upward and downward

The pattern and arrangement of note in a given scale is founded upon the three rules governing the identification of scales

Characteristics of a scale formula

  1. 1) fixed quantity of note

  2. a scale formula gives the quantity of note in the scale you intend forming

  3. 2) fixed interval between the notes found in the scale

  4. a scale formula gives the quality of the interval between the notes of the scale you intend forming

Forming Scales with Scale Pattern

There are four rules guiding the formation of scale us,they are

  1. the notes you use in the scale must be gotten from the 12 musical notes
  2. the fixed quantity of notes given in the scale formula must not be exceeded
  3. any quality of the interval contrary to that found between the notes of the scale formula would give another scale and not the intended scale
